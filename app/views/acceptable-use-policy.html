Acceptable use policy for artificial intelligence (AI) tools

{{ govukWarningText({ text: "You must follow this policy to protect government data and comply with UK GDPR and government security classifications.", iconFallbackText: "Warning" }) }}

Purpose

This policy tells you how to handle data when using artificial intelligence (AI) tools and applications in the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra).

Who this applies to

This policy applies to all digital professionals using AI tools in Defra.

Only use approved AI tools

You can only use AI tools that Defra has approved. These are:

  • Microsoft Copilot (Defra enterprise licence)
  • GitHub Copilot (Defra enterprise licence)

You cannot use any other AI tools.

Check data classifications

When using approved AI tools, you can only input data classified as 'OFFICIAL' or 'OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE'.

You must never input data classified as 'SECRET'.

Read the Government Security Classifications guidance to understand these classifications.

Do not use personal information

You must never enter any personal data or personally identifiable information (PII) into AI tools.

Read what counts as personal data on the ICO website.

You cannot use personal information even when:

  • using approved AI tools
  • data appears anonymised but the person could be re-identified
  • you have consent to process the data elsewhere

Review and updates

This policy will be reviewed every 6 months or when significant changes occur to:

  • government security policies
  • data protection legislation
  • approved AI tools and their capabilities
  • identified risks or incidents

Last updated: 17 September 2025
