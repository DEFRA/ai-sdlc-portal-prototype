Frequently asked questions

Find answers to common questions about using AI tools at Defra.

Getting started

Which AI tools can I use at Defra?

You can only use AI tools that have been approved by Defra:

  • Microsoft Copilot (Defra enterprise licence)
  • GitHub Copilot (Defra enterprise licence)

You cannot use any other AI tools without prior approval.

How do I get access to approved AI tools?

Contact your line manager or IT support team to request access to approved AI tools. You may need to complete training before gaining access.

Do I need training to use AI tools?

Yes, you must complete mandatory training on AI tool usage and data security before using any approved AI tools. This ensures you understand the acceptable use policy and data handling requirements.

Data and security

What data can I use with AI tools?

You can only input data classified as 'OFFICIAL' or 'OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE' into approved AI tools.

You must never input:

  • Data classified as 'SECRET'
  • Personally identifiable information (PII)

Can I use personal information with AI tools?

No, you must not input any personally identifiable information (PII) into AI tools. This includes names, email addresses, phone numbers, or any other data that could identify individuals.

This is to protect privacy and comply with UK GDPR requirements.

How do I know what data classification I'm working with?

Check the data classification markings on your documents or systems. If you're unsure, ask your line manager or information security team.

Read the Government Security Classifications guidance for more information.

Using AI effectively

Should I always trust AI-generated content?

No, you should always review and verify AI-generated content before using it. AI tools can make mistakes or provide inaccurate information.

Always:

  • Check facts and figures
  • Review for accuracy and relevance
  • Ensure content meets quality standards
  • Take responsibility for final outputs

Support and reporting

Who can I contact for help?

You can request support for help with licensing, training, or technical issues related to AI tools.

For urgent security concerns, contact the information security team immediately.

What should I do if I accidentally input sensitive data?

If you accidentally input sensitive data into an AI tool:

  1. Stop using the tool immediately
  2. Report the incident to your line manager
  3. Contact the information security team
  4. Follow your department's data breach procedures
